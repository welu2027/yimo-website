# Youth International Math Olympiad (YIMO)
YIMO is a global competition for middle and high school students designed with fairness, community, and growth as core values.

## Overview
- Event date: May 23/24, 2026
- Target participants: middle school and high school students
- Registration: teams (3–4) or individuals
- Core components: individual round, team round, guts round, tiebreakers

## Schedule
- Registration opens 12 weeks before event
- Pre-event: training materials, sample problems, and mock contests
- Event day(s): multi-paper competition, award ceremony
- Follow-up: results, certificates, sponsor prizes, community recognition

## Division structure
- Division 3: middle school (recommended)
- Division 2: early high school / advanced middle school
- Division 1: high school olympiad-level
- Alternative structure: div1/div2/div3 based on performance

## Problem format
- Individual round:
  - mixed MCQ + numerical-response (0-999) AIME-style
  - balanced difficulty (accessible and challenge)
- Team round:
  - AIME-type numeric questions
  - collaborative problem-solving
- Guts round:
  - timed answer sets (3×8) with immediate submission in rounds
- Tiebreaker round:
  - 2-3 hard problems; include proof/graph-theory-style reasoning

## Scoring & awards
- Overall placements: 1st, 2nd, 3rd
- Scholar tiers:
  - Gold Scholar: Top 10% (90–100)
  - Silver Scholar: 80–90
  - Bronze Scholar: 70–80
- Special awards:
  - Sponsor grand prize award(s) (e.g., $2k packages)
  - Sweepstake for top-scoring school/team
- Participation certificates:
  - Certificate of Merit
  - Honorable Mention
  - Certificate of Participation
